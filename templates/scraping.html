Scraping

Sometimes there comes a time when you want to automate an action, and there isn't an API, or perhaps the API is very limited, (Facebook and LinkedIn, I'm looking at you). When that happens, you can either say, "Oh well, I guess I'll do something else", or you can stop being a quitter for once in your life and scrape the site and automate browser input with tools like Mechanize or Selenium.

I know, I know. Scraping is bad and only mean people do it. Well, that may be true, but when I set out to do a task, I don't let things like 'Partner Programs' or lack of consideration from certain sites hold me back. If I want to programatically check my bank balances, or delete 50k terrible group members from my Facebook group (after spamming them with hundreds of pseudo biblical posts about the coming of the BanBot) because they can't follow a simple set of rules, I'm going to do it. (And I have)

Scraping is a little hacky and it is prone to breakage any time there is an update to the front end of the site, but it can be a very valuable tool, even if it does break every couple of weeks. There are countless tedious tasks that can be automated to your advantage with scraping. A retailer could monitor prices on a competitor site and send automated social media posts or emails that undercut the competitor's price. Scraping can monitor sites for bad reviews on a product or business so you can get a jump on customer satisfaction. The only limit to what you can scrape or bot is your imagination (and the occasional captcha)

If you find yourself in need of a data scraping or bot solution, short term or long term, shoot me an email. I love new challenges, and I am always on the lookout for projects and opportunities.
