Linux

I have been using Linux as a desktop OS since around 2010, which gave me a good knowledge base with basic commands, apt and dpkg package managers, and other common Linux knowledge. About 4 years ago, I got a little more serious about Linux as a career skill and begin to learn about other distros, process automation, and dove pretty deep into BASH scripting and cron jobs. As my skills have progressed, I have installed and configured many Linux systems (I refurbish old computers with Lubuntu and give them away), and picked up a great deal of troubleshooting knowledge.

Although I do most of my scripting in Python these days, I can still cobble together a BASH script to conjure all manner of of voodoo when the situation calls for it. I am a little behind the times with automation frameworks, but look forward to getting into Ansible, Chef, Puppet, etc as time allows.
