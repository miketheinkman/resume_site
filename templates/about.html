About Me

One of the biggest concerns that companies either have (or should have) is whether or not a new employee will be a culture fit. In that vein, I'd like to tell you a little about myself as a person to help you judge for yourself whether or not I would be a good addition to your team.

Overall, I am a very calm person. I deal with most things analytically. I am not really a fan of big shows of emotion and office politics. I have a pretty good sense of humor, and I like to joke around quite a bit. I don't really have any limits when it comes to humor, but I don't mind if others do. I can be at home in a reasonably sterile office environment or in an edgy startup with no dress code and a beer on every desk, though I do prefer something closer to the latter. In my opinion, decorum has its place for some roles, but in the trenches, I prefer function over form, and a casual environment.

I have basically no ego. I don't mind teaching someone a new skill if I can, and I have no problem learning a better way to get the job done. I don't like working with divas. I believe in respect in the workplace and in life. If you have a rockstar on your team who happens to also be a constant pain in the ass, I'm probably not a good fit, however if you have a good culture already, I'll be able to find my place in it, and hopefully bring some added humor with me.

In my personal life, I have a family, so I need a healthy work/life balance, but I don't expect or need lots of unscheduled time off. I have really enjoyed working remotely and having a flexible schedule in the past, but honestly I think I wind up overworking. If I have a list of things that need to be done, I take pride in taking care of it as quickly as I can while ensuring best practices. I guess that is good for my employer, but sometimes it can be taxing personally.

While I do need a good balance between work and recreation, part of my recreation is programming. I started coding because I enjoy it, and it has just grown from there. I like to play with open source software, home automation, Raspberry Pi, Arduino, etc. I enjoy what I do, and I don't see any burnout in the near future. I have had days where I start coding at six or seven in the morning, and don't stop (with the exception of a few short breaks) until after midnight.
